## ECF in Relation to ECC
ECF has always been developed "in-service" to ECC- I added stuff that was useful for us (Studio JUWL). The characters we made and the reason you can play those characters.
The general idea behind the "convenience" additions was always "Hey, if we're going to repeat adding this feature - if practical, we should put it in the framework".
Of course, there have been considerations for other devs who aren't us, but it wasn't a priority. I'm sure there are reasonable things we could add as I'm writing this, but it hasn't become relevant for us, quite yet.

Even despite being sort of one and the same, I really thought people would mix up the acronyms less...

## Personal Feelings
We've had our ups and downs, but the ride's been worth it. I'm happy to have something to send out.  

I hope the way I've written this framework isn't esoteric... Not that modding 3 A.I.R. and making characters isn't already.
We've spent a long time working on this project as a whole, and we've also spent a fair amount of times just being a friend group. (Which I think is more important than getting work done most of the time...)

## Looking Back
It's been a long time that I've been working on stuff for 3 A.I.R. (ever since scripting came out, and a little before that, actually...) - but I'm happy with what we've done.
I wanted to make Mighty and Ray for a long time, even before character frameworks were a thing. (Though, those were starry dreams... working on characters is tough work!)

ECF is something I thought about in August 2023, and it stuck in my mind for about a year or so. Just wanted to make my own framework that handled the back end with character IDs as text strings.
We originally didn't have ECF, but I decided I wanted to handle the back-end too. Thought it made more sense than relying on a system I didn't write.
April 9th, 2024 was when ECF actually started being created - it only took 4 days for a finished prototype... And we kept going from there, really.
December 2025, a whole year later, there I am, writing the manual for that framework.

## My Contribution
It was different and difficult being on such an important leading role, but I tried to do as much as I can to make by spot worthy. Many sprites, ideas, and even animation code was contributed by me. I couldn't have done it all myself so I am glad that help was there.

## Experience with a First-Time Project
This is actually the first time I've ever led a project and formed a team. Of course, Lemm was the one who worked on her own framework, while I handled how the characters should play out. You will see how differently we approached things from how you usually see character mods. We made them as if they were part of the base roster. We also wanted to treat fans and outsiders with the same respect by keeping things simple, adding some extra options for variety. I nearly feared people weren't going to like simple, but the public reception so far has been surprisingly great. I'm just glad I didn't have to stress myself doing things that didn't really needed to be done!

## Afterthoughts
I'm glad I could work on something to flex my artistic capabilites recreating and making new art for Sonic 3, and I'm glad playtesters liked it too!
I really hope people see Amy in particular and the framework made, and really get inspired to make something fun and simple. You are not forced to go all out like we did on the Mighty and Ray ports. Take your time on something you like and hope others enjoy it too.
There is no "correct" way to make your character. In fact, our Metal Sonic is going to be pretty diverse and short from the usual, planning to have his own layouts! Hope to see you all in the future when that happens!

## My history with the ECC/ECF project
I remember keeping a pretty close eye on Mia's work for a while now, and in 2023 I decided to help the team out (way before Juwl was a thing proper). ## The work I've put in
At times Mia was not feeling it when making certain assets, and most of those were larger and/or 3D-esque sprites (data select portraits, big sprites, corkscrew animations, ramps, and other miscellaneous ultraspecific doohickey animations for the characters). Other times, funnily enough, they were simpler sprites, such as Ray's walk, which I mostly created, or pre-rotated versions of the run/walk cycles (This mod has full support for it!). As such, at least someone had to take the wheel here, so at the end of the day I ended up becoming "second banana" of the spriting crew at Juwl (on ECC in particular). Not only that, but I helped out with some near-release work such as the supplementary material (promo art, logos etc.)
